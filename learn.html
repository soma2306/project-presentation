Glasgow

Glasgow is a port city on the River Clyde in Scotland's western Lowlands. It's famed for its Victorian and art nouveau architecture, a rich legacy of the city's 18th–20th-century prosperity due to trade and shipbuilding. Today it's a national cultural hub, home to institutions including the Scottish Opera, Scottish Ballet and National Theatre of Scotland, as well as acclaimed museums and a thriving music scene.

Edinburgh

Edinburgh is Scotland's compact, hilly capital. It has a medieval Old Town and elegant Georgian New Town with gardens and neoclassical buildings. Looming over the city is Edinburgh Castle, home to Scotland’s crown jewels and the Stone of Destiny, used in the coronation of Scottish rulers. Arthur’s Seat is an imposing peak in Holyrood Park with sweeping views, and Calton Hill is topped with monuments.

Aberdeen

Aberdeen is a port city in northeast Scotland, where the Dee and Don rivers meet the North Sea. With an offshore petroleum industry, the city is home to an international population. It's also known as the ‘Granite City’ for its many enduring grey-stone buildings. The 19th-century Marischal College is typical – a monumental Victorian landmark that’s now headquarters of the City Council.

loch

Scottish lochs are a key feature of the country’s breathtaking landscape. From the world-renowned mythical Loch Ness to the popular and picturesque Loch Lomond, these natural wonders are stunning to behold.Lochs are bodies of water. This is because the word ‘loch’ is derived from the Scottish and Irish Gaelic languages. Scottish lochs can come in a range of shapes and sizes.

Highlands

The Scottish Highlands are a mountainous region encompassing northwest Scotland. Loch Ness is overlooked by the ruins of medieval Urquhart Castle and known for mythical monster “Nessie”. Northeast, near the city of Inverness, dolphins swim in the Moray Firth. Southwest, in the Western Highlands, trails wind up Ben Nevis, the U.K.’s highest peak, and red deer roam Glencoe valley with its waterfalls.

Perth

Perth is a city beside the River Tay in central Scotland. The Fergusson Gallery, in a former circular water tower, features the paintings of 20th-century Scottish Colourist John Duncan Fergusson, and works by his wife, dancer Margaret Morris. The Black Watch Museum, at the Black Watch regiment’s ancestral home in Balhousie Castle, shows uniforms, medals, weapons and paintings.
